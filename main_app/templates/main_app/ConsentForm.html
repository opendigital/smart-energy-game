Study purpose: The purpose of this research is to learn about how people make energy-related decisions in a group environment.

What will I do? Your participation will involve answering some survey questions and completing a game in which you will make decisions as if you are a resident of a building trying to achieve a group energy conservation goal. Depending on your energy choices and those of others in your building, you will earn a smaller or larger financial incentive. Participation in this research project is voluntary and open to those aged 18 and older. You are free to stop participating at any time.

Study length: You can take approximately 60 minutes to complete the study.

Possible risks: Breach of confidentiality is a potential risk for all research. The researchers have taken actions to minimize this risk. The safeguards to prevent such a risk can be found in the Confidentiality section of the form. There are no additional risks from the study more serious than those in everyday life.

Potential benefits: There are no direct benefits to you for participating in this research study. There may be benefits to general knowledge about how consumers perceive and use energy devices.

Alternative: You may choose not to participate in this study.

Payment and other incentives: Depending on your decisions and those of the other participants in the task, you will earn a minimum of $0.50 from participating in this experiment. Depending on your and others’ choices, you may earn well above $0.50. The credit will be applied to your Amazon M-Turk account.

Confidentiality: You will not be asked to provide your name or any other identifying information during the study. Our HITs are external to Amazon’s servers. Data from these experiments will be retained electronically on a password protected computer, identified only by the Worker IDs (anonymized strings with no personal information), and accessed only by members of the research team. This data will be retained past the end of the project for replicability of any published results and possible future analysis. The project's research records may be reviewed by the National Science Foundation and by departments at Purdue University responsible for regulatory and research oversight .

My rights: Your participation in this study is voluntary. You may choose not to participate or, if you agree to participate, you can withdraw your participation at any time without penalty.

Contact: If you have questions, comments or concerns about this research project, you can talk to one of the researchers via email: researchopendigitalinnovation@gmail.com. If you have questions about your rights while taking part in the study or have concerns about the treatment of research participants, please call the Human Research Protection Program at (765) 494-5942, email (irb@purdue.edu).
